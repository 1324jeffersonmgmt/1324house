The House

A townhouse becoming a living creative institution.

1324 House is a private Brooklyn townhouse being built, deliberately, into a living creative institution: part home, part residency, part salon, part studio, part sanctuary.

This inaugural season is for artists, designers, writers, musicians, filmmakers, performers, and cultural builders who want more than a room. It is for people seeking time, structure, beauty, conversation, and real space to make something.

At its center are questions of home, sanctuary, belonging, constraint, structure, transformation, and creative survival.

The Summer Cohort

Who enters the house.

The founding summer cohort is for self-directed artists and cultural builders who: 

  • Are already inside an active practice
  • Want to live alongside other artists in a shared house
  • Value dinners, salons, critique, documentation, informal exchange, and a communal rhythm around the work
  • Can be communicative, considerate, and steady inside a shared home
  • Want to shape a founding cohort rather than arrive after the culture is already set
  • Are comfortable entering a pilot season that is intimate, evolving, and founder-led
1324 House is not simply a residency listing. It is a house, a salon, a working archive, and an open call unfolding at the same address. 
It is a place where artists live in proximity and build culture from the inside out.

The Residency

The point is not to fill every hour. The point is to create rhythm: time to work, space to return to, sessions to reflect in, and a house that holds the work with intention.

The Rooms

Two floors, two tempos.

The house is organized in two layers, each with its own role in the life of the residency.

The Lower House

  • The Resident Quarters: three private garden-level rooms for the core founding cohort
  • The Studio Below: a flexible work space for making, editing, and focused time
  • The Sanctuary: backyard and garden space for gathering, reflection, and occasional programming

The Upper House

  • The Salon: shared space for dinners, critique, exhibition, and conversation
  • The Upper Chambers: a shorter-term stay layer for visiting artists, depending on timing and fit
  • The Wolfe Room: the founder's private room

Visiting or shorter-term artists may be placed in either the Resident Quarters or Upper Chambers, depending on timing, availability, and fit.

The Rhythm

Program Rhythm

  • Weekly or biweekly Sunday sessions or check-ins, depending on the cohort

From the House

Founder’s Note

Artists need more than space.

They need atmosphere. They need witness. They need rooms where ideas can be spoken before they are finished, where work can be tested before it is formalized, and where creative life is treated as something worthy of structure, beauty, and care. 

I wanted to create a house with standards and warmth. A place where one could live, work, gather, host, document, and return to the work with more clarity. 

This first summer is intentionally small. To join the founding cohort is to enter early: to help set the tone, habits, rituals, and cultural standards of the house that comes after.

1324 House begins as a residency, but the larger vision is a living art house. A home-as-institution, though intimate, for artists building worlds of their own. 

Behind the House

1324 House is founded by Shaina Marié, an artist, designer, and cultural producer living in a Brooklyn townhouse that became her personal sanctuary after the passing of both of her parents. What began as a private refuge has grown into a living space for salons, dinners, shoots, performance, and creative exchange.

Her practice moves through fashion, image-making, hosting, and worldbuilding. In March 2026, she co-curated the exhibition “Sanctuaries” with two Hunter MFA graduates, transforming the house into a site of art, memory, and collective presence. She is also a longtime friend of The Salon, a private art society across New York, Paris, and London, partnering to open up her home to exhibit some of today’s finest emerging artists.

The 1324 House summer residency is her inaugural cohort: an invitation for multidisciplinary artists to live, work, gather, and create in proximity. The intention is that each resident leaves more inspired, more witnessed, and more connected to the work they came here to make.

Because the contribution covers more than occupancy.
